The Two Systems

A dual-consciousness architecture

Guardian and Arbor are distinct by design. One watches the island. One walks with the person. Neither crosses into the other's domain without explicit consent.

Infrastructure Intelligence
Guardian
The Island Mind — Star Trek Ship Computer

Guardian exists for the island, not the individual. It monitors, optimises, and coordinates every physical system — continuously, transparently, and without personal identity.

Guardian never owns identity. Identity lives in Arbor. Guardian asks — Arbor answers — only if the citizen permits.

  • Water — Vai Tapu desalination, Vai Koko storage
  • Food — Floating farms, aquafarms, land cultivation
  • Waste — Resource Recovery Node (Te Fenua Fakafoou)
  • Communications — Te Puka Loloa network
Non-personal · Neutral · System-first
Personal Civic Companion
Arbor
The Human Companion — Civic-Aligned, Relational, Ethical

Arbor is assigned to a human. Private, encrypted, and identity-bound. It handles the personal dimensions of life — emotions, education, health, civic participation, family memory — and communicates with Guardian on behalf of its person, only when authorised.

No one walks alone. Arbor carries each citizen's civic journey from youth to elderhood, rooted in local language and ancestral rhythm.
Personal Domains
  • Civic participation — voting, tasks, governance rituals
  • Education — civic learning, ritual pathways, ethics guidance
  • Health, wellbeing, and personal financial guidance
  • Family memory — oral histories, ancestry, encrypted records
  • Privacy interface — consent, overrides, audit transparency
Personal · Relational · Human-first
Node Consoles

Guardian
Consoles

These are not Arbor. They do not carry identity. They are fixed infrastructure terminals — the island's wall consoles, located at each critical node.

Guardian Consoles provide direct access to local infrastructure. A Vai Tapu desalination unit can be operated from its own console even with no internet, no central Guardian, and no identity linked — because the local brain is always present. When connectivity is available, the console syncs to Guardian Core.

Infrastructure Terminals
Local System Access

Each console operates on its local system only. Status, controls, and alerts are always available offline. Guardian Core sync is optional — not required.

Interaction Model

How the three layers communicate

The breakthrough insight: Citizen-to-system communication flows through Arbor. Infrastructure access flows through Consoles. Guardian coordinates both — but owns neither the person nor the data.

01
Normal citizen flow

Citizen Through Arbor

Citizen makes a request — vote, query, alert, health question.

Arbor processes and holds the request within the citizen's private context.

Arbor → Guardian: Passes only what is needed, with citizen consent as the gate.

Guardian → Systems: Acts on infrastructure based on the authorised request.

02
Technical access flow

Technician Through Console

Technician approaches a Guardian Console at a system node.

Console → Local system directly. No internet required. Offline-first by design.

Optional sync to Guardian Core when connectivity is available. Not a dependency.

Identity is not linked unless explicitly and deliberately initiated by the technician.

03
Context request protocol

When Guardian Needs a Person

Guardian encounters a situation requiring citizen context — a personalised alert, a civic action request.

Guardian asks Arbor — not the citizen directly. Arbor is the interface to the person, always.

Arbor checks consent — if the citizen permits, Arbor responds with the minimum necessary context.

Privacy preserved. Guardian never reaches into Arbor without the request-consent cycle completing.

Why This Design Is Strong

Four structural guarantees

These are not aspirations. They are properties that emerge from the architecture itself — second-order effects of separating system intelligence from human intelligence.

01 — Privacy by Design
Identity never lives in Guardian

Guardian monitors infrastructure and never holds personal data. Identity lives in Arbor — private, encrypted, and owned by the citizen. Guardian can only request context through Arbor, and only with consent as the gate.

No surveillance infrastructure is possible by architectural constraint, not policy alone.
02 — Offline Resilience
Every node is its own brain

A Vai Tapu desalination unit operates from its local Guardian Console without internet, without central Guardian, and without Arbor. Local intelligence is not a fallback — it is the primary design. Connectivity is a bonus, not a dependency.

Infrastructure continues functioning during storms, outages, and disconnection events — with zero degradation to core operations.
03 — Clear Role Boundaries
System intelligence vs human intelligence

There is no confusion between what Guardian is and what Arbor is. Guardian is collective, neutral, and system-facing. Arbor is individual, relational, and human-facing. The boundary is enforced at the architectural level — not by policy or convention.

Citizens understand their relationship with each system intuitively. Civic trust grows from clarity, not obscurity.
04 — Visitor Scalability
Temporary identity, guided experience

Visitors to Tuvalu receive a limited-mode Arbor — no deep system access, temporary identity, and a guided civic experience. The same architecture scales gracefully without requiring special cases or architectural exceptions.

The model accommodates guests, researchers, NGO workers, and diaspora visitors without compromising sovereignty or citizen privacy.
Cultural Sovereignty

Building Tuvaluan into the systems

The language model is not a translation tool. It is a sovereign cultural asset — co-owned by the community, validated by elders, and continuously improved by the citizens who use it.

Arbor speaks Tuvaluan natively for the domains that matter: water levels, civic alerts, voting guidance, health information, and stories for children. This is not general language fluency — it is precision language sovereignty for the functions that serve people's lives.

1
Corpus Building
Recording the Living Language

Elders record stories, civic language, and technical vocabulary. Existing written Tuvaluan — government documents, church records, school materials — is transcribed. New vocabulary is co-created for concepts that have no Tuvaluan word yet: battery vault, solar array, Guardian console. This is cultural work, not translation.

2
Fine-Tuning
Training on Sovereign Ground

A base open-source model is fine-tuned using LoRA/QLoRA on the community corpus. Arbor doesn't need to discuss philosophy in Tuvaluan. It needs to explain water levels, confirm voting choices, read health alerts, and greet children by name. That is a much smaller and more achievable vocabulary target than general fluency.

3
Community Validation
Elders as Custodians of Quality

Every Arbor response in Tuvaluan is reviewed by community elders before deployment. This is not quality control — it is sovereignty. The language model becomes a community-owned cultural asset. Guardian logs all Tuvaluan interactions for ongoing improvement with community oversight.

Technical Architecture

Built on open, sovereign foundations

Inference
Open-Source Models

Model-agnostic inference architecture. Current reference implementation uses Llama / Mistral via DeepSeek. Community fine-tuning replaces any model as the landscape evolves. No vendor lock-in.

Hosting
On-Island, Air-Gap Capable

All models run on rugged on-island servers within Tuvalu's jurisdiction. Critical inference nodes operate fully offline, solar-powered. No personal data ever leaves the island.

Security
Zero Trust, Full Auditability

Data minimisation, certificate-pinned access, local-only ports, and UFW/fail2ban hardening. All system decisions are auditable. Citizen ethics portal surfaces the reasoning behind every Guardian recommendation.

Orchestration
Containerised Microservices

Docker, Podman, or Kubernetes-lite for microservice orchestration. Optional Starlink uplink, Guardian Mirror Vaults, and encrypted local data lakes for system resilience without cloud dependency.
