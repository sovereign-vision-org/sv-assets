The Brain

What the
datacenter does.

The hybrid nodes are Tuvalu's nervous system — collecting signal, broadcasting voice, relaying data. The datacenter is the brain — holding memory, running intelligence, and coordinating response. Each operates independently. Together they constitute a sovereign digital nation.

Guardian Core
Full AI orchestration

The datacenter runs Guardian's full intelligence — far beyond what the edge nodes carry locally. Predictive maintenance modelling, cross-system anomaly detection, telemetry pattern analysis, and national infrastructure dashboards all live here. Guardian Core receives heartbeats from every node every 60 seconds and maintains a continuous picture of the entire national infrastructure stack.

Arbor Voice Services
Civic AI in Tuvaluan

Arbor — the civic voice companion — runs its language model inference on the datacenter's GPU cluster. Citizens asking questions at kiosk terminals, operators accessing system status, youth stewards following maintenance guidance: all of these interactions route through the datacenter's AI layer. No foreign API. No data leaves the island.

Telemetry Platform
All sensors. One view.

Every sensor across the island — water quality, soil moisture, air quality, energy production, battery state-of-charge, tidal level, and communications signal strength — feeds into the datacenter's telemetry stack. Prometheus ingests, Grafana visualises, Loki indexes logs, Alertmanager routes alerts. The national infrastructure becomes visible, measurable, and governable.

National Knowledge
Memory as sovereignty

Engineering documentation, maintenance manuals, policy records, cultural heritage archives, language preservation data, and civic history all reside in the datacenter's knowledge layer. A vector database makes this retrievable by Guardian and Arbor. When a steward asks Arbor how to service a desalination node, the answer comes from Tuvalu's own knowledge — not the internet.

compute cluster.

National Knowledge

Memory as
sovereignty.

The datacenter holds Tuvalu's collective intelligence — not just infrastructure data, but language, culture, history, and civic memory. When that knowledge is retrievable by Guardian and Arbor, sovereignty becomes operational, not symbolic.

01
Knowledge Archive
Engineering & Operations

Full technical documentation for every system in the Sovereign Vision stack — desalination, energy, waste recovery, communications, and civic infrastructure. Maintenance manuals, component specifications, commissioning records, and fault history. Indexed and retrievable by Guardian and Arbor when stewards need guidance.

02
Cultural Memory
Language & Heritage

Tuvaluan language corpus, oral history recordings, elder teaching archives, ceremonial records, and generational knowledge that predates written documentation. This layer ensures that Arbor speaks with cultural authenticity — not as a foreign assistant, but as a system trained on Tuvaluan ways of knowing.

03
AI Knowledge Retrieval
Vector Database

A vector database enables semantic retrieval across all knowledge layers. Guardian and Arbor query by meaning, not keyword — finding the relevant maintenance procedure, the relevant cultural precedent, or the relevant policy record with a natural language request. The retrieval system operates entirely on-island, with no external dependency.

Continuity Architecture

Sovereignty
beyond the island.

The datacenter is the brain — but brains can be lost. Mirror Vault architecture ensures that Tuvalu's knowledge, governance records, and system state survive even the total loss of the primary datacenter. Distributed replication to geographically separated vault locations — including future oil-rig anchor sites — means continuity is not contingent on any single location.

Continuous replication

All audit logs, configuration snapshots, AI model states, Guardian rule sets, and cultural archive data replicate continuously to mirror vault locations. No manual backup required — replication is a constant background process.

Geographic distribution

Mirror vaults are positioned at geographically separate locations — future sites include oil-rig anchor platforms and allied partner facilities. No single climate event or infrastructure failure can reach all vault locations simultaneously.

Governance continuity

Even in the event of a complete datacenter loss, the nation's governance records, civic decisions, cultural memory, and infrastructure knowledge remain intact and recoverable. Sovereignty is preserved independent of any single physical site.

Two racks.
One brain.

The sovereign datacenter occupies two physical server racks. Rack 1 hosts all network, control, and platform service infrastructure. Rack 2 carries the GPU compute cluster and the full storage estate. Both racks are powered by the same on-island solar-battery grid that feeds the Te Puka Loloa nodes.
