What This Network Does

Six reasons
this matters.

Te Puka Loloa is more than an antenna. It is a commitment — to connection, to safety, to self-determination. Here is what every node actually does for the people of Tuvalu.

1
Feso'ota'i
Connection
It brings everyone online

Every family, school, clinic, and business in range of a node can make calls, send messages, and access the internet — using their existing phone, with no foreign carrier required.

  • Public Wi-Fi access for phones and devices
  • Mobile network — calls, texts, and data
  • Internet access for schools and families
  • No SIM contract or foreign carrier needed
"This is what connects the island — so everyone can talk, learn, and reach the world."
2
Puipuiga
Emergency Lifeline
It works when everything else fails

When a cyclone hits, power goes out, or the sea rises, this node stays operational. It runs on battery power for over six hours with no solar. It cannot be switched off from outside.

  • Continues operating through power outages
  • Satellite backup when fibre is cut
  • Mesh relay to neighbouring nodes
  • Emergency radio broadcast capability
"Even during a cyclone, this node keeps communication alive."
3
Nofoaga Lautele
Community Access Point
A digital village hub

Each node creates a zone of civic access — free Wi-Fi, Arbor terminals, and digital services for schools, clinics, and community spaces. It is infrastructure built around people, not profit.

  • Free public Wi-Fi zones near each node
  • Access to Arbor civic services and guidance
  • Supports schools, clinics, and civic areas
  • Guardian terminals for stewards and staff
"A place where the island comes together, digitally."
4
Mata'itu
Local Intelligence
It helps the island think and respond

Every node is a listening post. It collects environmental data — weather, power levels, water pressure, air quality — and feeds that information to Guardian, which coordinates the island's response in real time.

  • Weather and environmental monitoring
  • Water system status — Vai Tapu and Vai Koko
  • Energy grid health and battery state
  • Feeds data to Guardian for coordination
"It helps the island understand itself and respond in real time."
5
Tuto'atasi
Resilience & Independence
Owned by the island

Every component is locally controlled, locally maintainable, and open-source. There is no foreign server that can be switched off, no carrier that can raise prices, and no company that can revoke access.

  • Locally controlled — no foreign dependency
  • Open-source software — fully auditable
  • Works offline when external links are down
  • Maintained by trained local stewards
"This is Tuvalu's network — not something controlled from outside."
6
Lumana'i
Future Platform
It prepares the next generation

This network is the foundation on which everything else is built — online education, remote work, digital commerce, AI civic services, and a growing digital economy governed by Tuvaluans for Tuvaluans.

  • Online education and remote learning
  • Remote work and digital services
  • Foundation for smart infrastructure
  • AI civic systems — Guardian and Arbor
"This is how the next generation connects to opportunity."

Puipuiga · Emergency Lifeline

Designed for
the worst day.

Tuvalu lives with cyclones, king tides, and power outages. The network was designed to assume these conditions are normal — and remain operational through all of them.

Always on
Mobile Core + Guardian Edge
Calls, emergency data, and Guardian monitoring never switch off — even when battery drops to critical reserve.
Satellite fallback
LEO Uplink — automatic
If the fibre spine or 60 GHz bridge goes down, the satellite link activates automatically. No manual switching required.
Emergency broadcast
VHF/UHF Civic Radio
A dedicated VHF/UHF antenna broadcasts emergency alerts even if all digital tiers are offline. The last channel never closes.
Self-powered
Solar + Battery Autonomy
6.4 kW rooftop solar with a 28–30 kWh battery bank. Critical services run for 6+ hours with no sunlight whatsoever.
Signal tier autonomy — battery-only operation
Tier 1 · Mobile Core + Guardian 6+ hrs
Tier 2 · Satellite Uplink 4–5 hrs
Tier 3 · Community Wi-Fi 2–3 hrs
Tier 4 · Emergency Radio Always on
When battery falls below 35% or Guardian detects a storm alert, non-essential load sheds automatically — in priority order — to extend the life of critical communications as long as possible. Stewards can override through Arbor at any time.
Tuto'atasi · Independence

Maintained by
Tuvaluans.

Every node is designed to be inspected, repaired, and operated by trained local stewards — not foreign engineers. Arbor guides each task in Tuvaluan, step by step.

"We did not design this for a company to own.
We designed it for a community to keep."

Youth Workforce
Trained stewards at every node
Young Tuvaluans are trained and paid as certified node stewards. They inspect hardware, check sensor data, and carry out maintenance guided by Arbor — in Tuvaluan. This is not a social programme. It is a structural maintenance budget line.
Arbor Guidance
Instructions in Tuvaluan — step by step
Arbor, the civic AI companion, delivers maintenance instructions, fault diagnoses, and system status in plain Tuvaluan. No technical jargon. No foreign language required. The knowledge lives in the system — the steward just needs to follow it.
Naming Ceremony
Each node is given a name
Every node is formally named in a community ceremony — giving it identity, ownership, and cultural weight. A named node is not just hardware. It is a member of the community that the community will protect and care for across generations.
Open Source
Every component is auditable
All software is open-source and published. Any technical partner, any government ministry, any curious steward can inspect exactly how the system works. There are no black boxes. Sovereignty requires transparency.
Mata'itu · Local Intelligence

The nervous
system connects.

Every node in the network feeds data to Guardian — which uses it to coordinate water, food, energy, and civic services. The communications network is the connective tissue that makes the rest of the system possible.

Water · Vai Tapu
Desalination & Water Storage

Sensors at each Vai Tapu desalination unit publish live water pressure, flow rate, and tank levels over the mesh. Guardian monitors for faults and alerts stewards before supply is affected.

Energy · ArborMesh Grid
Power Generation & Storage

Solar output, battery state-of-charge, and grid load data flow through the network continuously. Guardian uses this to balance power across the island and shed load intelligently during shortfalls.

Civic · Arbor
Citizen Interface

Arbor terminals in civic spaces connect through the mesh to Guardian Core. Citizens ask questions, stewards receive guidance, and community governance data flows — all through this network.

Intelligence · Datacenter
Guardian Core & AI Services

Three backhaul paths connect every node to the sovereign datacenter — carrying Guardian intelligence, Arbor AI services, telemetry analysis, and Mirror Vault replication between the edge and the brain.

Lumana'i · Future Platform

Phased across
the archipelago.

The network grows with the nation — beginning in Funafuti, reaching every outer island, and eventually integrating with the undersea cable that connects Tuvalu to the world. Each phase builds on the last.

01
Phase 1 · Funafuti Core
Three Nodes — Civic and Critical Sites

Three nodes cover Funafuti's key civic sites — the desalination hub, the school cluster, and the main civic centre. The private mobile core launches. Youth stewards are trained and certified. Guardian Edge begins monitoring-only operation.

02
Phase 2 · Outer Islands
Archipelago-Wide Expansion

The mesh extends to outer islands and maritime docks. Each island cluster receives a satellite-enabled node for off-island uplink. Phase 1 stewards take primary inspection roles. Arbor begins issuing guidance in Tuvaluan.

03
Phase 3 · Fibre Integration
Undersea Cable Connection

Integration with the Vaka undersea cable network. Pre-provisioned fibre ports on every existing node activate with no hardware change. Satellite transitions to a resilience-only backup role. The island joins the global internet on its own terms.
