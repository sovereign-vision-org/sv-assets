Design Principles

Built different.
By design.

01
Modular by nature

Every system is independently deployable. A village can start with a single desalination node and a solar array. The rest can follow when resources allow. Nothing requires the whole stack to function — but everything is designed to integrate when the time comes.

02
Sovereign by default

No foreign cloud. No external carrier contract. No proprietary lock-in. Every system runs on open-source software, locally-held hardware, and community-controlled governance. Guardian's audit trail belongs to Tuvalu — not to any vendor or platform.

03
Culturally load-bearing

Tuvaluan naming, ritual integration, blessing ceremonies, and Arbor interfaces in Te Gana Tuvalu are not decorative. They are the adoption mechanism. A system the community does not recognize as their own will not be maintained, defended, or improved by them.

AI Stewardship

Guardian watches
over all of it.

Guardian is not a product. It is a civic covenant — an AI framework embedded directly into Tuvalu's infrastructure, governed by the community, and answerable to the people it serves.

Monitors energy, water, food, comms, and waste simultaneously
